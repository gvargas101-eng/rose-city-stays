Short-term and corporate rental company in Tyler, TX. Brand colors: pink/mauve + green. 10 properties. Target: leisure travelers, healthcare workers, corporate guests.

## Idea A: "East Texas Warmth" — Organic Editorial

**Design Movement:** Organic Modernism meets Southern Editorial (think Kinfolk magazine meets boutique hotel branding)

**Core Principles:**
1. Warmth over polish — earthy tones, tactile textures, imperfect beauty
2. Editorial asymmetry — content bleeds past grid, text wraps around imagery
3. Generous whitespace as hospitality — breathing room signals luxury
4. Handcrafted details — subtle grain overlays, hand-drawn dividers

**Color Philosophy:** Warm cream (#F5EFE6) background with deep forest green (#2D5016) for structure, dusty rose (#C9847A) as accent. The palette evokes East Texas pine forests and rose gardens — grounded, welcoming, never corporate.

**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial layout. Hero section has full-bleed image offset to the right with large serif headline anchored left. Property cards use a masonry-style irregular grid. Sections alternate between full-bleed and contained widths.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin botanical line illustrations as section dividers
2. Oversized pull-quotes from guest reviews
3. Warm grain/noise texture overlay on hero sections

**Interaction Philosophy:** Slow, deliberate hover transitions (400ms ease). Cards lift gently with a warm shadow. Navigation links underline from center outward.

**Animation:** Fade-up entrance animations on scroll (staggered 80ms delay per card). Hero headline splits by word with a 60ms stagger. No jarring motion.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (serif, italic for emphasis)
- Body: DM Sans (clean, modern, readable)
- Accent: Playfair Display SC (small caps for labels)

## Idea B: "Rose City Luxe" — Contemporary Boutique Hotel

**Design Movement:** New Luxury Minimalism — think 1 Hotel or Soho House brand aesthetic

**Core Principles:**
1. Confident restraint — less is more, every element earns its place
2. Monochromatic depth — tonal layering instead of color contrast
3. Typography as architecture — headlines as structural elements
4. Precision spacing — 8pt grid, mathematical proportions

**Color Philosophy:** Off-white (#FAFAF8) base with charcoal (#1C1C1A) for text. Dusty mauve (#B8869A) as the sole accent — used sparingly for CTAs and highlights. The restraint makes every pink element feel intentional and premium.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-viewport hero with a single centered statement. Below the fold, a strict two-column layout alternates image-left/text-right. Property grid uses uniform 3-column cards with identical aspect ratios. Navigation is minimal top bar, no hamburger on desktop.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin horizontal rules (1px) as section separators
2. Property names in large, light-weight display type
3. Subtle background texture using a linen/paper noise pattern

**Interaction Philosophy:** Everything transitions at exactly 250ms cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1). Hover states reveal information rather than transform shape. Booking CTA pulses once on page load to draw attention.

**Animation:** Clip-path reveals on scroll (content slides in from a masked edge). No bounce, no spring — only smooth, linear elegance.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Cormorant Garamond (ultra-light for large sizes, medium for body)
- Body: Outfit (geometric, clean, modern)
- Labels: Outfit Uppercase with 0.15em letter-spacing

## Idea C: "Tyler Bloom" — Vibrant Southern Hospitality

**Design Movement:** Maximalist Southern Chic — bold color, pattern, and personality

**Core Principles:**
1. Color as personality — the brand's pink and green are celebrated, not toned down
2. Layered richness — overlapping elements, bold borders, decorative frames
3. Warmth through abundance — full content, rich imagery, generous copy
4. Community feel — testimonials and local highlights are first-class citizens

**Color Philosophy:** Deep rose (#A0395A) as primary, sage green (#5A7A4A) as secondary, warm ivory (#FDF6EE) as background. Gold (#C9A84C) as a tertiary accent for stars and highlights. The palette is unabashedly Southern and feminine without being frivolous.

**Layout Paradigm:** Stacked full-width sections with alternating colored backgrounds. Hero uses a diagonal split between image and color block. Property cards have bold colored top borders. A "Why Book Direct" section uses a 4-column icon grid.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Diagonal color splits between sections
2. Bold card borders in brand colors
3. Decorative rose motif (SVG) used as a watermark in hero

**Interaction Philosophy:** Energetic hover states — cards scale up 2%, buttons fill with color from left. The site feels alive and inviting.

**Animation:** Slide-in from sides on scroll. Staggered card reveals. CTA button has a shimmer effect on hover.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Fraunces (variable, high-contrast serif with personality)
- Body: Nunito (rounded, friendly, approachable)
- Accent: Fraunces Italic for pull-quotes

## Selected Approach: **Idea B — "Rose City Luxe" (Contemporary Boutique Hotel)**

Rationale: The existing site already uses pink/mauve and green, but in a fairly generic Hostaway template. This new site should elevate the brand to feel like a premium boutique hotel — confident, restrained, and memorable. The dusty mauve accent honors the existing brand color while the overall aesthetic signals quality to corporate and leisure travelers alike.
